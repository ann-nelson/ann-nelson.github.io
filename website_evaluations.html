Web Evaluation

Good Website: Apple

Apple’s website features a sleek, modern design with simple visuals, easy navigation, and a consistent color scheme.

  • C: Uses bold typography, large product images, and a clean background to ensure readability and appeal.
  • R: Keeps fonts, colors, and layout consistent across pages to reinforce brand identity.
  • A: Follows a grid layout for clear, professional alignment of text, images, and buttons.
  • P: Organizes related information to help users find what they need quickly.

Apple’s website is a prime example in effective web design, prioritizing user experience and aesthetics.

Bad Website: LingsCars

LingsCars website features a bold, cluttered design with bright colors, lots of animations, and an overwhelming amount of text and images.

  • C: Poor contrast due to clashing colors and excessive animation, making key information hard to focus on.
  • R: Inconsistent fonts, colors, and design elements create a jarring experience.
  • A: Misaligned text and images make the site look cluttered.
  • P: Overcrowded sections reduce readability and usability.

Ling’s Cars overwhelms visitors with an assault of visual clutter and disorganization, making it a prime example of what not to do in web design.
